Why Dog Gelato

Not just a treat.
A better way to spoil them.

Dog Gelato was built on a simple idea: dogs deserve a frozen treat made from real, recognizable ingredients — not a repurposed human dessert. Here's what makes it different, for the dogs who eat it and the people who sell it.

The Problem

Most frozen treats aren't made for dogs

Ice cream, dairy-based "pup cups," and sugary novelty treats are built for human taste buds — not canine digestion. Dog Gelato exists because dogs deserved better.

🥛

Dairy & Lactose

Most dogs are lactose intolerant. Traditional ice cream and dairy-based treats can cause upset stomachs and discomfort.

🍬

Added Sugar

Sugary desserts offer empty calories with no nutritional benefit — and can contribute to long-term health issues.

❓

Mystery Ingredients

Vague labels and fillers make it hard for pet parents to know what they're actually feeding their dog.

Real ingredients flat-lay — strawberries, banana, peanut butter
Health & Safety

Built from real, recognizable ingredients

Every flavor starts with whole ingredients your dog already knows and loves — no mystery powders, no dairy, no added sugar.

  • ✓ All-natural ingredients, clearly labeled — real strawberries, banana, peanut butter, mint, and vanilla
  • ✓ Lactose-free and gentle on digestion
  • ✓ No added sugar — sweetness comes from the fruit itself
  • ✓ Portioned per cup, so pet parents always know how much they're serving
The Process

Simple to make. Easy to love.

No freezer aisle, no cold chain, no fuss. Just three steps between the pantry and a happy tail.

1

Mix.

Add water to one cup of Dog Gelato powder. No complicated preparation, no special equipment.

2

Freeze.

Pour into the cup and freeze for 4–6 hours. A little patience, a lot of excitement.

3

Love.

Serve and enjoy together. The best moments are always shared.
